What is ADHD and how is it diagnosed in India?
ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental condition affecting focus, impulse control, and activity levels. In India, ADHD is diagnosed by qualified psychiatrists through clinical interviews, symptom checklists such as the ASRS, and behavioral assessments. Is ADHD common in Indian adults?
Yes, ADHD affects approximately 2-5% of adults in India, though it is significantly underdiagnosed. Many Indian adults with ADHD struggle with focus, productivity, and relationships without knowing the root cause. What are the symptoms of ADHD?
Common ADHD symptoms include: difficulty focusing on tasks, trouble organizing work, forgetfulness, fidgeting or restlessness, impulsive decision-making, difficulty sitting still, interrupting others, and trouble completing projects.
